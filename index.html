Desenvolvimentos de AI no contexto clínico

“However, the literature does not typically address the effectiveness of clinical AI solutions from the perspective of human users” […]

“Past work on systems using artificial intelligence, such as computer-aided detection (CAD) or Decision Support Tools (DSTs) has highlighted several obstacles in going from research and development environments to hospital or clinical settings. These obstacles include frequent lack of utility to clinicians and logistical hurdles that slow or block deployment . Even systems with widespread adoption, such as CAD in Mammography, have been shown to require a radiologist to do more work, not less, and generally do not improve a radiologist’s diagnostic accuracy.” […]

“The rapid and widespread development of DL methods supports a wide range of image analysis tasks in breast cancer diagnosis, including classification, detection, and segmentation . These methods rely on large annotated datasets to learn essential and discriminative image features for each specific task, with performances matching and even surpassing humans . However, past works are highlighting several obstacles in going from research and development environments to hospital or real clinical settings for these set of applications . The lack of utility to clinicians and logistical hurdles that slow or block deployment are frequent obstacles in real clinical settings . Even systems with widespread adoption, such as systems to aid radiologists during breast cancer diagnosis, are requiring them to do more work . Instead of reducing the radiologist workload or ease the clinical workflow, these systems are generally not improving the radiologist’s diagnostic accuracy.” […] There is extensive literature on the design of clinical decision support system and computer aided diagnosis tools. Despite the widespread development of commercially available medical imaging frameworks there is limited adoption of these tools. The academic consesus for many years for this failure was the lacking performance of these systems, but with the introduction of deep learning techniques the paradigm shifted to explain this failure as a disconnect from the HCI field. The intersection of these recent advances and the HCI is an open area of study better known as HAII.

BreastScreening-AI

“The proposed BreastScreening framework incorporates an assisted tool, as it is able to integrate an AI model for breast cancer classification and segmentation. This assisted tool offers radiologists an autonomous second reader opinion during the breast cancer diagnosis. Specifically, we are using a DenseNet model. To validate the proposed DenseNet results, our framework allows the radiologist to accept or reject the suggested BI-RADS classification. Therefore, the radiologist can freely control the final diagnosis result. The framework operates as a website accessed via web browser.” […]
